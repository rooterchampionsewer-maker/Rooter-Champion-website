How We Install

3 REPLACEMENT OPTIONS

Rusted, collapsing, or failing drains? We’ll replace them the way that makes the most sense for your home.

Option 01 · Full Replacement

FULL REPLACEMENT

When rusted cast iron or galvanized drains have reached the end of their life, we remove them completely and install brand-new lines underneath your home — a permanent fix, not a patch.

Includes:
  • Full removal of old pipes
  • New drain lines under home
  • Main sewer reconnection
  • Permits & inspections handled
Option 02 · Redrain / Reroute

REDRAIN / REROUTE

Instead of tearing up your foundation, we design a new pathway that bypasses the damaged drains and connects to your main line. Fix the problem permanently — without the destruction.

Includes:
  • New routed drain pathway
  • No digging inside the home
  • Tie-in to main sewer line
  • Minimal disruption to living space
Option 03 · Section Replacement

SECTION REPLACEMENT

When only part of your drain system has failed, we target just the damaged section and replace it with new pipe — saving you money while solving the problem at its source.

Includes:
  • Targeted pipe replacement
  • Camera-verified scope
  • Tie-in to existing drains
  • Cost-effective fix

Not sure which option fits your home? Call 714-305-9263 — we’ll camera-inspect your drains and lay out the options.

What A Redrain Looks Like

NEW PIPES. NEW PATH.
NO DIGGING INSIDE.

NO DIGGING THROUGH YOUR HOME. When your old drains have failed, we don’t tear up your foundation to get to them. Instead, we design a completely new pathway — routing fresh drain lines around the damage and connecting them directly to your main sewer line.

BUILT TO LAST. Every redrain is installed with modern PVC pipe, proper slope calculations, and code-compliant connections. These aren’t patches — they’re permanent new drain lines designed to outlast the originals by decades.

YOUR HOME STAYS INTACT. We work around your existing foundation, flooring, and landscaping. The old pipes stay where they are — we simply bypass them with a new route that flows clean and drains right. Most redrains are completed in a day or two.

That’s what makes a redrain the smart choice when your drains are too far gone for lining or repair — a fresh start without the demolition.

Is This Right For You?

SIGNS YOU NEED REPLACEMENT

If your home is showing any of these symptoms, your drains may be at the end of their life.

Rusted Cast Iron

Old cast iron pipes that have corroded from the inside out — flaking, scaling, and losing capacity.

Recurring Backups

Drains clog again and again no matter how many times you snake them — the pipes themselves are failing.

Slab Leaks

Water or sewage showing up under your foundation — a clear sign the drains beneath your home have failed.

Cracked Or Collapsed Pipe

Camera inspection reveals broken sections, offset joints, or collapsed pipe that can’t be repaired.

Old Galvanized Lines

Homes built before the 1980s often have outdated drain materials that have reached the end of their life.

Failed Trenchless Candidate

Pipes too damaged for lining or bursting? A full replacement or reroute is the long-term solution.
