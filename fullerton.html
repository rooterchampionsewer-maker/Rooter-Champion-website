Common Plumbing Issues in Fullerton

WHAT WE SEE IN FULLERTON HOMES

Aging Pipes in Historic Downtown Homes

Fullerton's historic district near Harbor and Commonwealth has beautiful 1920s Craftsman bungalows and Spanish Revivals — but the original galvanized water lines and cast iron drains are now a century old. Low pressure, rust-colored water, and recurring drain backups are the early signs.

Our fix: Camera inspection to confirm pipe condition, then targeted repipe or trenchless lining for sewer mains. We respect historic finishes — no unnecessary demo.

Hillside Drainage in Sunny Hills & Raymond Hills

Properties up the hills have sloped lots that complicate sewer grade. Improperly pitched lines, settled connections after years of seasonal soil movement, and rainy-season backflow are common in these neighborhoods.

Our fix: HD camera diagnostic with elevation mapping. Trenchless lining or re-grade where needed — no torn-up landscaping.

High-Use Drains in CSUF-Area Rentals

Student rental properties around Cal State Fullerton see heavy plumbing use. Wipes, paper towels, hair, and grease in kitchen lines lead to frequent clogs. Mainline backups are common at turnover season.

Our fix: Hydro jetting clears the whole line at once. We also offer landlord maintenance plans — scheduled cleanings between tenants so you don't get the emergency call.

Root Intrusion Under Old Sycamores & Oaks

Fullerton's mature tree-lined streets — sycamores, oaks, magnolias — are beautiful but their roots find every crack in older clay sewer lines. If your home backs up every few months, this is almost certainly the cause.

Our fix: Robotic root cutting for immediate relief, then trenchless lining for a permanent fix — no excavation through your front lawn.
