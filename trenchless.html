Choose The Right Method

2 TRENCHLESS OPTIONS

Depending on the condition of your pipe, we use one of two trenchless repair methods.

Option 01 · Repair From Inside

TRENCHLESS LINING

A flexible resin-saturated liner is inserted into your existing pipe and cured in place. Creates a seamless, jointless new pipe-within-a-pipe — stronger than the original and lasting 50+ years.

Best for:
  • Cracked or fractured pipes
  • Root intrusion & leaks
  • Deteriorated cast iron & clay
  • Offset joints
  • Typically completed in 1–2 days
Option 02 · Full Replacement

PIPE BURSTING

When pipes are too damaged to line, we replace them entirely — without digging. A bursting head fractures the old pipe as it pulls in a new HDPE pipe behind it. Same-diameter or upsized replacement.

Best for:
  • Collapsed or severely damaged pipe
  • Bellied or sagging lines
  • Need to upsize pipe diameter
  • Full pipe replacement needed
  • Typically completed in a single day

Not sure which one you need? Call 714-305-9263 — we’ll run a camera inspection first to determine the right method.

Is This Right For You?

SIGNS YOU NEED TRENCHLESS REPAIR

If you’re dealing with any of these issues, your sewer line likely needs repair or replacement.

Recurring Clogs

Drain cleaning isn’t holding — clogs keep coming back because the pipe itself is damaged.

Sewer Line Leak

Damp spots in your yard or foul odors coming from the ground indicate a broken pipe.

Tree Root Intrusion

Roots growing into your pipe through cracks or joints are a sign of pipe deterioration.

Sewage Backup

Sewage backing into your home usually means the main line is collapsed or severely blocked.

Old Cast Iron or Clay Pipe

Homes built before 1980 often have deteriorated pipes that need full rehabilitation.

Camera Shows Damage

A camera inspection has revealed cracks, offsets, or severe corrosion in your line.
